$$\int \sqrt{81 - x^2} \, dx$$

Ejemplo 1

Consideremos la integral:

\(\int \sqrt{81 - x^2} \, dx\)

\(\sin \theta = \frac{x}{9}\)

\(x = 9 \sin \theta\)

\(x^2 = 81 \sin^2 \theta\)

\(dx = 9 \cos \theta \, d\theta\)

\(\int \sqrt{81 - x^2} \, dx = \int \sqrt{81 - 81 \sin^2 \theta} \cdot 9 \cos \theta \, d\theta\)

\(= \int \sqrt{81(1 - \sin^2 \theta)} \cdot 9 \cos \theta \, d\theta\)

\(= \int \sqrt{81 \cos^2 \theta} \cdot 9 \cos \theta \, d\theta\)

\(= \int 81 \cos^2 \theta \, d\theta\)

\(\cos^2 \theta = \frac{1}{2} (\cos 2\theta + 1)\)

\(81 \int \frac{1}{2} (\cos 2\theta + 1) \, d\theta\)

\(= \frac{81}{2} \int (\cos 2\theta + 1) \, d\theta\)

\(= \frac{81}{2} \left( \int \cos 2\theta \, d\theta + \int d\theta \right)\)

\(= \frac{81}{2} \left( \frac{1}{2} \sin 2\theta + \theta \right) + C\)

\(= \frac{81}{4} \sin 2\theta + \frac{81}{2} \theta + C\)
